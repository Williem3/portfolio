About me


Java | Spring Framework | Thymeleaf
Hibernate | REST | SQL | HTML | CSS

Since I was 8 years old, I have always been fascinated by computers.


I took this fascination with me into my first college programming course and failed. My first actual failure, which led me to believe I was not cut out for providing a computer instructions on what to do. I spent sometime figuring out what career field interested me after this. Eventually, I realized that I would never be satisfied in life if I have set limitations on what I could do.


The first web application I made was an employee/customer relationship manager. Inb order to get to the main page, a user must register and login. Once signed in managers and admins could see/add/delete/update any employee/customer. While regular employees could only see/delete/update customers. This project taught me a lot in regard to basic CRUD development.


From this point on I realized that I no longer had limitations set one myself. I took this momentum and built an Online Banking web application that maintained transactions, accounts, appointments, and authentication. At the same time, a user who has registered may make a deposit or withdrawal, or even send funds to someone else if they have their email. This project helped solidify what I learned in CRUD development and allowed me to learn other useful tools.


What I'm getting at here, is that I'm someone who is willing to break past his walls and get to the meat of the problem. Doesn't matter how difficult or how much time it may take. I'm willing to go the extra mile.


I look forward to seeing where I am a year from now.
